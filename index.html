OSCARAgricultural Mapping UAV
Frame850mm Custom CF
Motors4114 320KV
Battery6S 5000mAh
Est. Flight25 – 30 min
Overview

OSCAR is an endurance-optimized agricultural mapping quadcopter built on a custom-designed 850mm carbon fiber frame. The entire software stack — MAVLink integration, distance-based camera trigger, geotagging, NDVI processing, and WebODM API pipeline — was written from scratch. Not a kit build — every subsystem was designed, built, and coded by me.

Frame Design

16mm round carbon fiber tubing (2mm wall) with 3D-printed PETG-CF structural connectors. Round tube enables internal wire routing — all motor phase wires run through the hollow arm center, zero exposed wiring. Motor mounts are round clamp-style blocks gripping 45mm of arm tube. Nylon M3 bolts on center plates for electrical isolation. Steel M3 on motor mounts for structural clamping — a deliberate materials decision.

NDVI Imaging Pipeline

Raspberry Pi Zero 2W with NoIR Camera v2 and 680nm bandpass filter. Haversine distance-based capture fires every 5m of GPS ground track — accounts for speed variation and crosswind. Images geotagged in real-time from MAVLink GPS. NDVI formula (NIR − Red)/(NIR + Red) applied per pixel. 680nm filter isolates infrared for ~$3 instead of a $400 multispectral camera.

TypeCustom Rocket
AvionicsSelf-Designed

Overview

STRATO is a custom-built rocket with a fully self-designed avionics system. Flight computer is a LilyGO T-Beam V1.2 — ESP32 with integrated GPS, LoRa, and OLED display. The entire structural design was modeled in Onshape, simulated in OpenRocket, physically built, and launched.

Rocket Design

Ogive nose cone for aerodynamic efficiency, custom clipped delta fins cut from balsa wood and sanded to an aero profile. Stability coefficient tuned to 1.9–2.0 cal — below 3.47 (over-stable) which risks turning aggressively into wind. Length: 105cm. Calculated apogee: ~559m. Blue and white livery inspired by BPS.space's Avalanche rocket.

Self-Designed Avionics

Avionics bay designed from scratch in Onshape with dedicated brackets. Full flight state machine: IDLE → LAUNCH_DETECT → BOOST → COAST → APOGEE → DESCENT → LANDED — triggered by BMP280 altitude deltas. Key problems solved: TX/RX solder bridges causing silent download failures; ESP32-CAM voltage mismatch (OV3660 runs 2.8V — swapped to OV2640); altitude averaging to smooth ±0.3m barometric noise.

Overview

Real-time GPS hiking tracker built on a LilyGO T-Beam running Meshtastic firmware. Connects to a phone hotspot and broadcasts live GPS over MQTT to a Python script on a laptop, which plots the trail live on a Leaflet.js map. No cell coverage required. Trail-tested with 10-satellite lock and 30-second position updates confirmed throughout a full walk.
