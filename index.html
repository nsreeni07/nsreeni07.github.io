I'm a self-taught engineer from South Brunswick, New Jersey with a passion for building hardware that actually works in the real world. I go from concept sketches to soldered circuits to something that flies — teaching myself everything along the way through trial, error, and a lot of research.

I'm drawn to the hardest parts of a problem: the mechanical design, the wiring decisions, the firmware that ties it all together. I don't just assemble kits — I design the parts, print them, build them, and iterate until they work. My projects span custom rockets, autonomous UAVs, and off-grid GPS tracking systems.

OSCARAgricultural Mapping UAV
Frame850mm Custom CF
Motors4114 320KV
Battery6S 5000mAh
Est. Flight25 – 30 min
Overview

OSCAR is an endurance-optimized agricultural mapping quadcopter built on a custom-designed 850mm carbon fiber frame. The entire software stack — MAVLink integration, distance-based camera trigger, geotagging, NDVI processing, and WebODM API pipeline — was written from scratch.

This is not a kit build. Every subsystem — frame, gimbal, software, and imaging pipeline — was designed, built, and coded by me.

Frame Design

The frame uses 16mm round carbon fiber tubing (2mm wall) and 3D-printed PETG-CF structural connectors. Round tube was chosen specifically to enable internal wire routing — all motor phase wires run through the hollow arm center from the ESC stack to the motor tip, resulting in zero exposed wiring on any arm.

Motor mounts are round clamp-style blocks gripping 45mm of arm tube with M3 bolts through heat-set inserts. Nylon M3 bolts are used on center plates for electrical isolation and vibration damping. Steel M3 bolts handle motor mount clamping load — a deliberate materials decision.

Imaging Payload & Passive Gimbal

The payload is a Raspberry Pi Zero 2W with a NoIR Pi Camera v2 and a 680nm bandpass filter for NDVI vegetation health approximation. All images are geotagged in real-time from a MAVLink GPS stream over serial.

Camera stabilization uses a custom single-axis passive pendulum gimbal — no motors, no electronics. A wooden dowel through two F688 ball bearings lets gravity keep the camera nadir regardless of drone pitch. Pendulum length was calculated to keep its natural oscillation frequency below INAV's pitch correction band, preventing resonance.

STRATORocket Flight Computer
PlatformLilyGO T-Beam
MCUESP32
CommsLoRa 915MHz
StatusComplete
Overview

STRATO is a custom hobby rocket built around a LilyGO T-Beam V1.2, integrating an ESP32 microcontroller, GPS module, LoRa radio, and OLED display on a single board.

The system features a full flight state machine — detecting launch, boost, coast, apogee, descent, and landing phases using BMP280 barometric altitude. Live telemetry transmits via LoRa to a handheld ground receiver. An ESP32-CAM captures onboard video.

Key Challenges Solved

USB cable: Initial flashing failures traced to a charge-only USB cable with no data lines. Classic first-build trap.

Download mode: Required holding IO38 + RST simultaneously to enter the ESP32 download mode for flashing.

I2C bus: OLED at 0x3C and BMP280 at 0x76 on a shared bus — OLED driver identified as SH1106, not the more common SSD1306. Wrong driver = blank screen.

TX/RX pin conflicts: Resolved UART pin conflicts between LoRa, GPS, and serial debug on the T-Beam's limited pin layout.

TrailBeamReal-Time GPS Hiking Tracker
PlatformLilyGO T-Beam
FirmwareMeshtastic
DashboardLeaflet.js
ProtocolMQTT
Overview

TrailBeam is a real-time GPS hiking tracker designed to work entirely without cell coverage. A LilyGO T-Beam running Meshtastic firmware transmits GPS position packets over LoRa radio. A single-board WiFi/MQTT architecture receives the packets and feeds them to a Leaflet.js live map dashboard.

GPS lock and MQTT broker connection have been confirmed. Currently fine-tuning the minimum distance broadcast threshold to balance battery life against position update frequency.
